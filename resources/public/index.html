Gaiwan

We are a provider of technological expertise grown out of the consulting and development work of Arne Brasseur. Our job is to find a path from a business need to a technical solution that ensures we can deliver quality and reliability within time and budget. To do so we are highly opinionated when it comes to choosing the software components we build on, and we work closely with customers to come up with pragmatic solutions that work.

Our values

We are a remote-first company

Working on-line forces us to write things down, to document what we do. We default to communicating asynchronously, but use synchronous communication for its higher bandwidth when it makes sense.

We value collaboration

We encourage pair programming, seeking feedback, and working together to solve problems. Being remote-first makes it even more important to actively involve colleagues, to share knowledge, to be a team.

People first

Gaiwan is not a company that optimizes for profit above all else. Instead our goal is to meaningfully impact the lives of the people we work with, to remunerate them fairly, to allow them to work and live in a way that energizes them instead of draining their energy, and to work together with good people on interesting and challenging projects.

We value healthy, sustainable profitability because it allows us to provide these things.

We work the system

We value documented, repeatable processes. While the core of our work is unpredictable creative problem solving, a lot of what we do is recurring and mechanical. We want this mechanical aspect of our work to run like a well oiled machine, so that it gets out of our way, and we can maximize the space available for focused, creative work.

We believe in synergy through community and open source

We actively support the community we come from, through the hosting and moderating of online spaces, the organizing of offline events, the active participating in existing fora and networks, and the direct contribution in the form of open source software.

This is the community that shaped us, and in terms we help shape this community to be open, welcoming, diverse, and active. We become who we are in and through interaction with others. Community is a non-zero sum game, and every investment we make in it tends to return multifold over time. This is where are future colleagues and clients are, where we forge connections and friendships, and where together we change the face of the industry.

Our clients

Nextjournal is offering a Data Science product that aims to solve some of the issues faced by scientific publishing.

We have worked with Nextjournal for a large part of their journey, providing consulting to make sure their team could stay on track. This has included tooling improvements, architectural feedback, improvements to their testing approaches, and one-on-one coaching sessions with their developers.

Eleven is on a mission to end suffering in the world of accounting. They are building an ambitious new accounting product that needs to lift accounting practices into the 21st century.

Eleven chose the business intelligence tool Metabase so they could put flexible querying capabilities into the hands of their clients. Gaiwan was comissioned to create the Datomic database driver for Metabase, and worked with Eleven to integrate Metabase into their system.

The German non-profit Mein Grundeinkommen strives for the introduction of basic income.

We assisted Mein Grundeinkommen with the construction and load testing of the systems which select their campaign beneficiaries, as well as creating an internal tool for them called Marimba, which allowed them to run their mass email campaigns efficiently with a fraction of the cost.

Our projects

Lambda Island is our technical brand, aimed at developers who want to become better at what they do. It forms a key part of the Gaiwan success formula. By constantly researching deep technical topics and making them easily digestible we build up a knowledge and expertise in-house, which directly improves our client work.

All Gaiwan engineers are active open source contributors, and we have several open source libraries we created and maintain, available through the Lambda Island brand.

These projects often stem from needs we saw in client projects, or in the community in general. By opening them up we make sure we can use these building blocks on subsequent projects. It also enables contributions from other members of the technical community.
